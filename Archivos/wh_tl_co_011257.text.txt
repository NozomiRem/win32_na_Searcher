text01 - The enemies that appear in an Extreme Quest are artificial reproductions.<br><br>As such, dropped items which were difficult for the simulation to accurately<br>reproduce appear in an unstable state.<br><br>These items are denoted with a unique icon.
text02 - Items marked with this icon do not go directly to your inventory but instead <br>are categorized as unstable data and stored in a special virtual space.<br><br><yellow>When the quest is cleared, you can select a set number of them from the<br>Extreme Terminal to take out of VR with you. All other item data is lost<br>when the quest is ended.<c>

text04 - There is one Stage Order which dictates that the player must not receive<br>healing during a stage.<br><br><yellow>However, healing derived from a mag, automatic healing from skills,<br>and healing from paid items are not subject to this restriction<c>.
text05 - To further the Extreme Quest's manufacturing of high-stress situations,<br>enemies are artificially enhanced via a method called Capability Refactoring.<br><br>Capability Refactoring, which strengthens one of an enemy's resistances,<br>can be reconfigured by accomplishing the conditions of the previous stage's<br>Stage Order. This reconfiguration can result in a number of beneficial effects.
